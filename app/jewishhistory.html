Jewish History Explorer

A map and timeline of the Jewish people across ~5,000 years — where they lived and how many, how and why they moved, and the events that shaped them, from ancient Mesopotamia to today. The globe is shaded by Jewish population over time; arcs trace the great migrations; pins mark the major events.

The signature timeline runs along two tracks. The upper Record is what archaeology, inscriptions, documents and demography show. The lower Tradition is what the Tanakh, Talmud, liturgy and memory recount. Click any event to see both sides and a verdict — corroborated, partly, contested, or no direct evidence — where they meet and where they part.

Please read: this app is evidence-forward and even-handed. It reports the scholarly consensus on what the record supports; it does not set out to "debunk", and it treats the tradition with respect as a parallel layer of memory. Pre-modern figures are illustrative scholarly estimates; the map uses modern borders at every era as a visualization device, and (for example) splits the Russian Empire's Jewry across today's nations.

Sources: Sergio DellaPergola / American Jewish Year Book · Pew Research Center · Jewish Virtual Library · Encyclopaedia Judaica · the archaeological & documentary record (Merneptah & Tel Dan stelae, Lachish, Sennacherib's Prism, the Cyrus Cylinder, the Al-Yahudu tablets, the Dead Sea Scrolls, the Cairo Geniza…). Borders: Natural Earth. Globe: globe.gl.
