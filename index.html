What it does

Two tracks, side by side

Every event lives on the record (archaeology, inscriptions, documents) or the tradition (Tanakh, Talmud, memory) — or both, linked where they corroborate. Click one for a verdict: corroborated, partly, contested, or no direct evidence.

5,000 years on a globe

Spin a 3D globe (or a flat map) shaded by each country's Jewish population over time. Drag the deep-time slider from ancient Mesopotamia to today — or press play.

The great migrations

Animated arcs trace the movements and why — Assyrian and Babylonian exiles, the Roman dispersion, 1492, the great migration to America, the Holocaust, the ingathering to Israel.

Every event, judged

Nearly 400 events from the Patriarchs to today, each leading with the evidence and annotating the scholarly consensus — even-handed, never out to "debunk", with sources.

Population over time

Click any country for its Jewish population trend across the eras, the events that happened there, and who arrived or left. Toggle population, density, or share of world Jewry.

Search & share

Search any country to fly to it, and copy a link to an exact moment — an era, a country, an event — to share what you've found.

About the data & the stance

This explorer is evidence-forward and even-handed: it reports the scholarly consensus on what the record supports — corroborated, contested, or unattested — and treats the tradition with respect as a parallel layer of memory. Modern figures (1880–2025) draw on Sergio DellaPergola / the American Jewish Year Book, Pew Research and the Jewish Virtual Library; pre-modern figures are best-effort scholarly estimates. Figures are approximate and illustrative, and the map uses modern national borders at every era as a visualisation device — ancient peoples and polities, not today's nations, occupied that land. Country outlines: Natural Earth (public domain). Globe: globe.gl.
